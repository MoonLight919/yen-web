Temperature is measured in Celsius
Pressure is measured in hectopascals (hPa). 1 atm = 1,013.25 hPa
  • Over 1022.68 hPa (Anticyclone) - is generally considered high, and high pressure is associated with clear skies and calm weather
  • 1009.14 - 1022.68 hPa - can be considered normal, and normal pressure is associated with steady weather
  • Under 1009.14 hPa (Depression) - is generally considered low, and low pressure is associated with warm air and rainstorms
Humidity is measured in percentages (0% - 100%)
Wind speed is measured in meters per second (m/s)
  • 0 m/s (Calm) - Calm wind. Smoke rises vertically with little if any drift
  • 0 - 1 m/s (Light Air) - Direction of wind shown by smoke drift, not by wind vanes. Little if any movement with flags. Wind barely moves tree leaves
  • 1 - 3 m/s (Light Breeze) - Wind felt on face. Leaves rustle and small twigs move. Ordinary wind vanes move
  • 3 - 5 m/s (Gentle Breeze) - Leaves and small twigs in constant motion. Wind blows up dry leaves from the ground. Flags are extended out
  • 5 - 8 m/s (Moderate Breeze) - Wind moves small branches. Wind raises dust and loose paper from the ground and drives them along
  • 8 - 11 m/s (Fresh Breeze) - Large branches and small trees in leaf begin to sway. Crested wavelets form on inland lakes and large rivers
  • 11 - 14 m/s (Strong Breeze) - Large branches in continuous motion. Whistling sounds heard in overhead or nearby power and telephone lines. Umbrellas used with difficulty
  • 14 - 17 m/s (Near Gale) - Whole trees in motion. Inconvenience felt when walking against the wind
  • 17 - 21 m/s (Gale) - Wind breaks twigs and small branches. Wind generally impedes walking
  • 21 - 24 m/s (Strong Gale) - Structural damage occurs, such as chimney covers, roofing tiles blown off, and television antennas damaged. Ground is littered with many small twigs and broken branches
  • 24 - 28 m/s (Whole Gale) - Considerable structural damage occurs, especially on roofs. Small trees may be blown over and uprooted
  • 28 - 33 m/s (Storm Force) - Widespread damage occurs. Larger trees blown over and uprooted
  • Over 33 m/s (Hurricane Force) - Severe and extensive damage. Roofs can be peeled off. Windows broken. Trees uprooted. RVs and small mobile homes overturned. Moving automobiles can be pushed off the roadways
